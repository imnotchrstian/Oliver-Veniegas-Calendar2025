Holy Saturday

April 19, 2025

Holy Saturday in the Philippines is the day between Good Friday and Easter Sunday, marking the final day of the Holy Week observances. It is a day of mourning and reflection as Filipinos await the resurrection of Jesus Christ. The day is often a quiet one, where many Filipinos reflect on Christ's sacrifice and prepare for the joyous celebration of His resurrection the following day.

Holy Saturday is also a time for church activities. In the evening, many Filipinos participate in the Salubong, a traditional pre-dawn procession that celebrates the meeting of the Virgin Mary and the risen Christ. This ceremony is often held in churches across the country, symbolizing the triumph of life over death. It is followed by a Mass of the Resurrection, marking the beginning of Easter Sunday.

Throughout Holy Saturday, Filipinos also observe the quiet reverence of the day. Shops remain closed, and the atmosphere is generally somber. People spend time in prayer, meditation, and family gatherings, waiting in anticipation for the celebration of the resurrection of Jesus Christ on Easter Sunday.
