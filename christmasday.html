Christmas Day

December 25, 2025

Christmas Day, celebrated on December 25, is the most anticipated and joyous holiday in the Philippines. As a predominantly Catholic country, Filipinos embrace the holiday with deep religious and cultural significance. The celebration begins as early as September, known as the start of the “Ber Months,” when homes, malls, and streets light up with festive decorations, Christmas trees, and the iconic Filipino parol (Christmas lanterns).

A key tradition during Christmas in the Philippines is attending the Misa de Gallo or Simbang Gabi, a series of nine dawn Masses leading up to Christmas Eve. On the night of December 24, families gather for Noche Buena, a grand midnight feast featuring dishes like lechon (roast pig), ham, queso de bola (cheese ball), and bibingka (rice cake). It is a time for family reunions, exchanging gifts, and spreading love and joy.

Christmas in the Philippines is more than just a day—it is a season of sharing, faith, and togetherness. Filipinos celebrate the holiday with caroling, parties, and charitable activities, embodying the true spirit of Christmas. The warmth and festivity make it a special time of the year, as families and communities come together to celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ.
