National Heroes Day

August 25, 2025

National Heroes Day is celebrated every last Monday of August in the Philippines to honor the courage, patriotism, and sacrifices of the country’s national heroes. The holiday is a tribute to those who fought for the Philippines’ independence and freedom, particularly those who stood up against colonization, oppression, and injustice. Among the most recognized figures are national heroes like Andres Bonifacio, Jose Rizal, and Emilio Aguinaldo.

On this day, Filipinos reflect on the legacy of these heroes, who made significant contributions to the nation’s history. Various ceremonies, flag-raising events, and wreath-laying activities are held in parks, monuments, and historical sites across the country. These events are intended to remind Filipinos of the sacrifices made by these heroes and inspire them to continue to serve their country with a sense of pride, patriotism, and unity.

National Heroes Day is not only about remembering the past but also about looking forward to a better future. It is a day to celebrate the values of freedom, justice, and equality while honoring the Filipino spirit of resilience and the pursuit of a just society. The holiday serves as a reminder that the struggles of the past continue to shape the Philippines' path toward progress and national unity.
