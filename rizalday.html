Rizal Day

December 30, 2025

Rizal Day is observed in the Philippines every December 30 to honor the life and heroism of Dr. José Rizal, the country's national hero. This public holiday commemorates his execution by Spanish authorities in 1896 at Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park in Manila), which became a turning point in the fight for Philippine independence.

The highlight of Rizal Day is the official ceremony at Rizal Park, where government officials, including the President, lead a wreath-laying ceremony at the Rizal Monument. Similar tributes and flag-raising ceremonies take place across the country, especially in places with historical ties to Rizal, such as his hometown of Calamba, Laguna.

Beyond the official observances, Rizal Day serves as a time for Filipinos to reflect on Rizal’s ideals of nationalism, education, and reform. Schools, organizations, and historians hold discussions and activities to remember his contributions. His works, particularly "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," continue to inspire Filipinos in their pursuit of freedom, justice, and progress.
