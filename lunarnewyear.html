Lunar Year Day

January 29, 2025

Lunar New Year, also known as Chinese New Year, is widely celebrated in the Philippines, especially in communities with strong Chinese influences. It marks the beginning of the lunar calendar and is a time for family reunions, feasting, and honoring traditions that symbolize prosperity and good fortune. Many Filipinos, regardless of heritage, participate in the festivities by decorating homes with red ornaments, lighting firecrackers to ward off bad luck, and preparing dishes believed to bring wealth and success.

In cities like Manila, particularly in Binondo—the world's oldest Chinatown—grand parades, dragon and lion dances, and temple visits create a lively atmosphere. People exchange gifts, such as tikoy (sticky rice cake), which signifies unity and strong family ties. Red envelopes, or ang pao, containing money are also given to children and unmarried individuals as a symbol of blessing and prosperity.

Beyond the celebrations, Lunar New Year in the Philippines reflects the country’s deep cultural diversity and appreciation for Chinese traditions. It is a time when Filipinos embrace the values of family, gratitude, and hope for a prosperous year ahead, blending their own customs with those of the Chinese-Filipino community.
